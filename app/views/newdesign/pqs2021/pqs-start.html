Pharmacy Quality Scheme 2021/2022

Use this service to complete your 2021/22 Pharmacy Quality Scheme (PQS) declaration.

The declaration closes on 25 February 2022 at 11:59pm.

Extension to complete PQS activity and collate evidence

To help ease the current pressures on community pharmacies, an extension for meeting the requirements of the Pharmacy Quality Scheme 2021/22 has been agreed.

Contractors now have from 1 April 2021 until 5 April 2022 to claim payments for the completion of a minimum of 20 completed New Medicine Service (NMS) provisions. This is a Gateway criterion where the end date was previously 5 January 2022.

For the other scheme requirements, contractors have until 31 March 2022 to complete the gateway criteria and domains and until 30 June 2022 to gather the evidence to demonstrate compliance, except for the following:

  • PCN Domain – this requires collaborative work on flu vaccination uptake which must be completed by 31 December 2021. The focus of the flu vaccinations is usually up to the end of the calendar year, so the deadline for this work will not be extended.
  • Advice on return of unwanted and used inhalers (Respiratory Domain) – the end of the period during which pharmacy teams should have conversations with patients dispensed an inhaler is still 31 January 2022.

There is no change to the declaration period - contractors must still complete and submit their declaration by 11:59pm on 25 February 2022. However, if contractors have been unable to complete the requirements for the gateway criteria or those in a domain by this date, as originally intended, they can make a declaration that they will complete the gateway criteria or domain by 31 March 2022 and that they will have the evidence to demonstrate compliance by 30 June 2022.

Payment Criteria

To be eligible for a PQS payment, you must achieve all of the following gateway criteria:

  • Advanced Services - New Medicine Service (NMS)
  • Safety report and demonstrable learnings from CPPE LASA e-learning
  • Risk review

You also need to provide information on the following domains:

  • Medical and Safety Optimisation
  • Respiratory
  • Digital
  • Primary Care Networks
  • Prevention
  • Addressing unwaranted variation in care
  • Healthy living support
