Pharmacy Quality Scheme

Risk management and safety

Did the pharmacy claim for the risk management criterion at the February 2019 QPS review point?

On the day of the current declaration does the pharmacy have available, at premises level, a risk review which includes two identified risks?

This must include:
  • An update of the previous risk review undertaken as part of the 2018/19 PQS to include a recorded reflection on the identified risk and the risk minimisation actions that the pharmacy team has been taking and any subsequent actions identified as a result of the reflection
  • the risk of missing sepsis identification and recorded demonstrable risk minimisation actions that have been undertaken.

On the day of the current declaration does the pharmacy have available, at premises level, a risk review which includes two identified risks?

This must include:
  • a risk in the pharmacy that has been identified and prioritised with identified risk minimisation actions that the pharmacy team is taking
  • the risk of missing sepsis identification and recorded demonstrable risk minimisation actions that have been undertaken.

Can the pharmacy demonstrate that they’ve actively identified and managed the risks at premises level associated with the specified Look-Alike Sound-Alike (LASA) medicines identified from the National Reporting and Learning System (NRLS)*?

(NHS Improvement’s top combinations of medicines by likelihood and harm caused are propranolol and prednisolone, amlodipine and amitriptyline, carbamazepine and carbimazole, rivaroxaban and rosuvastatin, atenolol and allopurinol.)

The pharmacy must have put in place actions to prevent these risks, and all actions identified in the patient safety report must have been implemented.

The pharmacy must upload any LASA incident reports to the NRLS and keep a record for confirmation of this activity at the pharmacy premises or within any electronic reporting system used. In the description of what happened in the NRLS report, the text ‘LASA’ must be included as a unique identifier to facilitate future national learning.

On the day of the declaration will the pharmacy have a new written safety report (Either written since the last review date, 15 February 2019, or covering the last year if not previously claimed.)

This must be at premises level and should include:
  • analysis of incidents and incident patterns taken from an on-going log
  • learning points from CPPE LASA e-learning
  • a review of incidents where mitigation has not prevented a LASA incident or near miss from happening and any actions taken as a result
  • evidence of sharing learning locally and nationally
  • actions taken in response to national patient safety alerts
