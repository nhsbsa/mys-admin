NSAID Audit

To meet the requirements for this audit you must:

  • collect data for 2 weeks
  • identify at least 10 patients who meet the criteria
  • add a maximum of 20 patients

If you cannot identify 10 patients in 2 weeks, your audit period will be extended by up to 2 weeks

If you cannot identify 10 patients at the end of the extended audit period (4 weeks), you will be able to submit your audit with less than 10 patients.

You do not need to input all entries at once. You can save your audit after adding each patient

Why we do this audit

Adverse effects of non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) including cyclo-oxygenase-2 (COX2) selective inhibitors are a frequent cause of preventable hospital admissions. The adverse effects include gastro-intestinal (GI) bleeds, pro-thrombotic events (e.g. heart attack, stroke) and reduced renal blood flow leading to acute kidney injury. This audit looks only at GI safety.

National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) guidance requires all patients prescribed oral NSAIDs for osteoarthritis or rheumatoid arthritis (no age limit) or for low back pain (if aged aged over 45) to be co-prescribed gastro-protection (e.g. proton pump inhibitor). All older patients (aged 65 or over) are at increased risk of NSAID induced GI adverse events. Additional risk factors include prolonged treatment and concomitant use of other other medicines which increase the likelihood of GI events. GI adverse events have also been reported where patients have self-medicated with a second NSAID in addition to the prescribed agent or have not taken a gastro-protective despite this being co-prescribed.

For full audit information please click here. This link also provides contact details for any questions regarding this audit. If you have any technical problems accessing/using the audit tool, please email nhsbsaresearch@nhs.net



Instructions

Include all patients aged 65 or over who present a prescription for any oral NSAID or COX2 inhibitor.

NSAIDs/COX2 inhibitors are in BNF chapter 10: Pain and inflammation in musculoskeletal disorders. This does not include patients prescribed aspirin.

Oral COX2 inhibitors and NSAIDs are listed in the guidance.
